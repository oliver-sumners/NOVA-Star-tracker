WHAT IS A QUATERNION?

A quaternion is a compact way to describe a 3D rotation — like saying "the camera is aimed this way in space." Four numbers replace three ambiguous angles and avoid the mathematical trap known as gimbal lock. The star tracker solves these four numbers fresh every frame by matching the star pattern it sees to its internal catalog.

CONVENTION USED HERE

This tracker outputs a passive ECI→body quaternion in Hamilton convention. That means it describes the rotation that takes a fixed star position in the sky (ECI frame — Earth-Centered Inertial) and transforms it into the camera's own coordinate system (body frame). The camera boresight points along the body +Z axis. This matches the Python QuestSolver output directly — same sign convention, same w≥0 enforcement.

WHAT EACH NUMBER MEANS
w Think of it like a "how much rotation" number. It is always between 0 and 1 here (we force w≥0). When w is close to 1, the camera is pointing close to its reference direction (Dec=0°, RA=0°). As the camera rotates further away, w decreases toward 0. You rarely interpret w alone — it works together with x, y, z. This matches exactly how your Python code enforces if q[0] < 0: q = -q. x Component of the rotation axis pointing toward the vernal equinox — RA=0h on the celestial equator (where the Sun crosses the equator in spring). In the passive convention, a large x means a significant rotation about that direction was needed to bring the ECI frame into alignment with the camera. y Component of the rotation axis pointing toward RA=6h (90° east along the equator). Together with x, these two components describe the "east-west" tilt of the camera boresight relative to the equatorial plane. z Component of the rotation axis pointing toward Earth's north celestial pole. When z is large, the camera boresight is aimed near the polar region of the sky. This is why z is large when pointing at Polaris or the Little Dipper.
Together [x, y, z] define the axis in ECI space the spacecraft rotated around to reach its current pointing, and w encodes how far it rotated. The camera boresight direction in ECI is always the third column of Rᵀ (body +Z expressed in ECI), which gives you the RA and Dec readout above.
